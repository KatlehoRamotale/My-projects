Terms, Conditions & Company Policies

These terms are intended as a general website and guest-policy draft for a South African hotel operation. They should be reviewed by a South African attorney before commercial use.

1. Bookings and Reservations

All reservations are subject to room availability, successful confirmation, and compliance with hotel policies. A booking is not final until the hotel issues confirmation through the website, email, or another accepted communication channel.

Guests must provide accurate booking details, including names, dates, contact details, and payment information where required. The hotel may cancel or refuse a booking if false, misleading, or incomplete information is supplied.

2. Pricing, Deposits and Payments

Prices displayed on the website are quoted in South African Rand (ZAR) and may change before a booking is confirmed. Once confirmed, the booking price recorded in the reservation system will apply unless a clear pricing error occurred.

The hotel may require a deposit, prepayment, or full payment before arrival depending on the booking type, promotional rate, or reservation terms selected by the guest.

3. Cancellations and Refunds

A guest may request cancellation of an advance booking, reservation, or order, subject to the hotel’s right to charge a reasonable cancellation fee where legally permitted. Refunds, where applicable, will be processed according to the booking terms, timing of cancellation, and any reasonable costs already incurred by the hotel.

In cases of death or hospitalisation affecting the booking, the hotel will consider cancellation and refund requests in line with applicable South African consumer law and supporting proof.

4. Check-in, Check-out and Guest Conduct

Guests must comply with published check-in and check-out times unless the hotel agrees otherwise in writing. The hotel reserves the right to charge additional fees for unauthorised late check-out, damage, missing property, or cleaning beyond normal room servicing.

Guests must behave lawfully, respectfully, and in a manner that does not endanger staff, other guests, or hotel property. The hotel may deny access, remove a guest, or cancel a stay where misconduct, threats, unlawful behaviour, or serious policy violations occur.

5. Privacy and Personal Information

The hotel processes personal information for reservations, guest communication, payment administration, customer support, legal compliance, and service delivery. By using the website and booking services, guests acknowledge that their personal information may be processed for these lawful purposes.

The hotel will take reasonable steps to protect personal information, limit access where appropriate, and retain information only as long as reasonably required for lawful business and legal purposes.

6. Website Use and Electronic Transactions

Online bookings, digital confirmations, and website communications may constitute electronic transactions. Guests are responsible for ensuring that the contact details they provide are correct and accessible for reservation notices, confirmations, and service-related communications.

The hotel may keep electronic records of bookings, terms acceptance, complaints, reviews, and other website interactions for operational, legal, and evidentiary purposes.

7. Complaints and Service Issues

Guests should raise service complaints as soon as reasonably possible so the hotel has a fair opportunity to investigate and resolve the issue. Complaints may be submitted through the website complaint section, reception, management, or any formal contact point published by the hotel.

Where a complaint remains unresolved, guests may also use external tourism or consumer complaint mechanisms available in South Africa.

8. Smoking, Safety and House Rules

Smoking is only permitted where clearly designated by the hotel and where legally permitted. Guests must comply with all smoke-free, fire-safety, security, and emergency rules applicable to the premises.

The hotel may impose cleaning fees, damage charges, or removal measures for smoking or related conduct in prohibited areas.

9. Liability and Property

Guests are responsible for their own valuables unless items are formally placed into an approved hotel safekeeping process where offered. The hotel will not be liable for loss or damage to guest property except where liability arises under applicable law and cannot lawfully be excluded.

Nothing in these terms is intended to remove or limit any consumer right that may not lawfully be waived or excluded under South African law.

10. Governing Law

These terms, hotel operations, and website services are governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Any disputes will be handled subject to applicable South African law, consumer rights, and competent forums or courts.

Legal Basis Used for This Draft

This draft is generally aligned to South African consumer protection, privacy, electronic transactions, tourism complaints, and smoking restrictions. It should still be reviewed and customised for your hotel’s exact business model, booking rules, cancellation policy, data handling, and provincial compliance needs.
